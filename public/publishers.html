Getting paid for creating content has never been easier. How does “do nothing” sound?

Publishers have been creating content for years and advertising has always paid for that content. Unfortunately, times are changing and web page ads are quickly becoming yesterdays news. In 2016 alone, Ad Blocking has risen 80%, reaching a new total of 500 million ad-blocked web surfers world wide. This growth is really putting a dent in publisher revenue from what was once a lucrative business solution.

Without making a change, this trend continues - gradually forcing smaller publishers to close up shop, and leaving large pubs to pick up the pieces with a shrinking staff.


Enter Brave Payments.


A new way to give users what they want - no more ads - and still give publishers a rev share that works, allowing them to continue delivering great content for the same reasons - just like it was 1959 all over again.

As Brave grows, so does your revenue. We expect between 5% and 10% of Brave users will contribute between 5 and 20 dollars per month to the publisher sites they visit most.

And here is the best part. Neither the user nor the publisher needs to do anything different. Just keep surfing. Just keep publishing.

Easy right?


Claim your publisher wallet.

Publisher wallets are being created every second. Each time a Brave user visits a site that qualifies as a publisher site, a new wallet is created automatically for that publisher. The wallet is held in escrow as it grows with micropayments. When contributions for an individual publisher are equal to about $100.00 USD (in BTC, of course), an email is sent to both the webmaster of the site and the registered domain owner. The email explains how to verify your bitcoin wallet with Brave Software.


Verification of a bitcoin wallet with Brave Software is a lot like verifying a domain name: Brave Software generates an unpredictable token and the publisher uses that token and their Bitcoin address to construct a TXT record in the DNS that Brave Software verifies.


  • Step 1: Contact your webmaster to reply to the email invitation from Brave which is titled "Brave Publisher Invitation", accepting your invitation to active your Brave Publisher Wallet.

  • Step 2: Receive a second email from Brave which includes a special TXT file that you will upload to your web server. This ensures a secure handshake between Brave and the server administrator which prevents someone from falsly claiming your wallet.

  • Step 3: Activate your wallet through Coinbase - and transfer some or all of it's balance back to any account that you wish to connect.
